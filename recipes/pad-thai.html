Pad Thai, a stir-fried noodle dish, is a beloved street food originating in Thailand. It became popular during the 1930s as part of a national campaign to promote Thai identity and reduce rice consumption. Today, it's enjoyed worldwide for its balanced flavors of sweet, salty, sour, and spicy.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/2 cup tofu or shrimp (optional)
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup chives (cut into 1-inch pieces)
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Crushed peanuts (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Steps

  1. Soak noodles in water until soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan, add garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant.
  3. Add tofu or shrimp and cook until done. Push to the side and add the egg, scrambling it.
  4. Combine noodles, tamarind paste, fish sauce, and sugar. Stir-fry until everything is well mixed.
  5. Add bean sprouts and chives, stir briefly, and remove from heat.
  6. Garnish with peanuts and lime wedges. Serve hot.

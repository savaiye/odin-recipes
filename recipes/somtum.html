Somtum

Discover Somtum: One of Thailand's Most Beloved Dishes

Somtum, also known as Thai green papaya salad, is a popular dish originating from the Isan region in northeastern Thailand. Traditionally made with shredded green papaya, this dish combines the four quintessential Thai flavorsâ€”spicy, sour, salty, and sweet. Somtum is believed to have been influenced by the culinary traditions of neighboring Laos and has become a staple in Thai cuisine over the past century.

Ingredients

  • Green Papaya (shredded): The main ingredient, providing a crunchy texture.
  • Garlic: Adds pungency and flavor.
  • Bird's Eye Chili Peppers: For spiciness (adjust quantity based on spice preference).
  • Fish Sauce: Adds saltiness and umami.
  • Lime Juice: Provides a fresh, tangy flavor.
  • Palm Sugar: Balances out the spicy and salty flavors with sweetness.
  • Cherry Tomatoes: Adds color and slight sweetness.
  • Long Beans (cut into small pieces): Adds texture and freshness.
  • Crushed Peanuts (optional): For additional crunch.
  • Dried Shrimp (optional): For a richer, umami flavo.

Steps

  1. Prepare Ingredients: Shred green papaya, slice tomatoes, and cut long beans into small pieces.
  2. Crush Garlic and Chilies: In a mortar and pestle, lightly pound the garlic and chilies until crushed.
  3. Add Flavorings: Add fish sauce, lime juice, and palm sugar to the mortar, and mix until combined.
  4. Mix Ingredients: Add the shredded papaya, tomatoes, long beans, and optional ingredients (dried shrimp, peanuts). Lightly pound and toss everything to combine flavors.
  5. Serve: Transfer to a plate and enjoy with sticky rice or grilled meats.
